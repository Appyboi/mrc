Marxism on the Question of Patriarchy: A Comprehensive Study

A Political Education Series by the Marxist Reading Circle

INTRODUCTION

Amidst intensifying Fascism and Imperialism that leaves no opportunity of persecuting the more marginalized sections of the working class at every instance, the Marxist Reading Circle announced Marxism on the Question of Patriarchy: A Comprehensive Study political education series in June 2025.

We aim to conduct a sincere and sensitive study of the subject from a Marxist-Leninist-Maoist perspective, understanding the need for a Marxist-Feminist approach that not only understands in-depth how Patriarchy is intertwined with Class Society but also condemns male-chauvinistic attitudes and practices in the Communist movement, along with building a revolutionary strategy for women’s, children’s and trans-queer liberation.

Find the resources read in the previous sessions in this document (updated every week).

Register for the upcoming reading sessions here

THE IDEA BEHIND THIS SERIES

Marxism on the Question of Patriarchy: A Comprehensive Study arose out of the simple thought that there is no one text we can read about patriarchy and claim that we have read sufficiently. The vastness of this topic inspired us to create a long reading list, enhanced further by participation of all existing comrades in this reading circle who suggested more resources and welcomed this series enthusiastically. Hence, it was like picking 50 out of 500 resources – some important texts and topics are bound to be left out. However it still covers over 50 resources and was bound to be a comprehensive series going on for sixteen months.

In today’s age of monopolist control of media where the Bourgeoisie thrashes the masses with social media algorithms and heightened focus on short “content” (TikToks/reels, memes, tweets) so as to plummet our abilities of engaging with political propaganda, there is a rising trend of short courses, crash courses, podcasts, etc. being the highest one can aspire to attend. We aim to break away from this trend with this comprehensive course aimed at understanding the subject thoroughly.

Besides, patriarchy is a sensitive subject as it is present in every aspect of social life, from what occupation one can have to the relations between parents and children.

Be assured: We are not studying about one monotonous topic again and again in a boring way, rather we are reading 40 different topics, all connected with the broad topic of patriarchy.

SECTIONS AND THEMES

This series is divided into the following eight sections based on related themes, as follows:

    Section 1 - Introduction: Reform or Revolution?

    We introduced the reading series by discussing the necessity for an anti-capitalist approach to women’s and trans-queer liberation by using our explanatory presentations as well as existing pamphlets and articles.

    THEMES:
  • A critique of liberal feminism (e.g. glass ceiling and rainbow capitalism)
  • Understanding the basics of Anti-Capitalist Feminism
  • Locating Patriarchy in Base and Superstructure
  • How classed is gender? How gendered is class?
  • A Marxist approach to the question of Patriarchy

    • NOTABLE AUTHORS:
    Cinzia Arruza, Tithi Bhattacharya and Nancy Fraser

    Section 2 - Gender and Sexuality in the Past

    Did you know that the first and largest group to become a part of the working class in the first stage of class society (slavery) were women? It is not a wholesome story, you have been warned.

    We found it absolutely important to study the formation of patriarchy and its interconnectedness with the formation of class society, to trace the origins of women, children and trans-queer people’s oppression along with understanding how patriarchy persisted in every mode of production and social order, along with its content and form in every stage.

    Do note that knowledge of history is utmost crucial for a Marxist understanding of any topic and hence rest all the sections contain sessions on history too. This section, however, contains fundamentals of a historical materialist understanding of patriarchy.

    THEMES:
  • Were we always like this? Sexual division of labour in primitive-communist society
  • How did women, transgender and queer people become oppressed?
  • Tracing the inception and growth of patriarchy in family, division of labour and modes of production across different stages of historical development of society
  • Understanding patriarchal exploitation of women’s economic, sexual and reproductive labour in Slavery and Feudalism
  • History of marriage and prostitution

    • NOTABLE AUTHORS:
    Frederick Engels, Gerda Lerner, August Bebel, Leslie Feinberg, Red Flag Switzerland

    Section 3 - Patriarchal Exploitation, Commodification and Subjugation

    In this section, we learnt our basics of understanding patriarchy in bourgeois society, a concept we explore in greater detail in Section 6 below. Here we introduced ourselves to the specific character of gendered division and exploitation of labour under capitalism, where women are exploited both as waged labourers in the social sphere and unpaid labourers in the domestic sphere. We also examined the bourgeois institution of marriage and discussed the need for transforming family relations in order to establish a socialist society. Moreover, we also studied men's robbing of the bodily autonomy of women under capitalism and the commodification of reproductive labour, i.e. prostitution and surrogacy.

    THEMES:
  • How the industrial revolution was founded upon the labour of women and children
  • Gendered division and exploitation of labour under Capitalism
  • Queer Work: gendered labour trajectories for trans-queer people under Capitalism
  • Domestic Work and Social Reproduction
  • Case Study: Patriachy in India under the conditions of semi-feudalism, semi-colonialism and the caste system
  • The Bourgeois institutions of marriage and divorce
  • The global flesh trade: prostitution and surrogacy
  • Understanding gendered violence

    • NOTABLE AUTHORS:
    Karl Marx, Frederick Engels, Nadezhda Krupskaya, Michelle O'Brien, Angela Davis, CPI(ML)[People's War], Esperanza Fonseca, Robyn Rowland

    Section 4 - Condemning the Naturalization of Sex and Gender

    Did you know that the dichotomy of the “biological sex” is actually a very narrow interpretation of human reproductive anatomy, that was purposely created by bourgeois scientists to “discover” justifications in nature for the oppression of women, at the expense of intersex people as well as women?

    In this section, we uncovered a lot of myths about intersexmisia/intersexphobia and transmisia/transphobia to avoid falling into the traps of bioessentialism. Note that in later sections, we distinguish between sexual and gendered exploitation and discuss the inseparability of the two while radically separating ourselves from bioessentialism.

  • How the dichotomous interpretation of biological sex is gendered
  • Myth bunking intersexmisia/intersexphobia
  • Were transgender identities "created"?
  • How transmisia/transphobia is a Class issue
  • The inevitable links of transmisia/transphobia with racism, misogyny, fascism and imperialism
  • Understanding gender as a sociocultural construct
  • Condemning transmisia/transphobia in left spaces
  • How the working class is not a monolith and socialism is not a zero-sum game

    • NOTABLE AUTHORS:
    Red Fightback, Sage (from the Marxist Reading Circle)

    Section 5 - Masculinity, Femininity and the Gender Ideology

    “Gender is a sociocultural construct that maintains and reinforces the patriarchal oppression in class society, i.e. economic exploitation, control on sexual and reproductive capacities and sociocultural oppression of women (and resultantly of transgender and queer people too). Although the narrative around which the cultural construct of gender is created is that of biological sex, the real material roots of gender lie in the system of patriarchy whose own roots are of an economic nature; since the role of women in childbearing, childrearing, domestic labour, etc. is primarily to create and sustain labour-power for capital to exploit.” (Quote from our study circle’s original treatise on gender)

    In this section, we understood how patriarchy operates in the ideological superstructure of bourgeois society, right from the otherization of women to motherhood and children’s oppression as well as from sexuality to gender transition.

    THEMES:
  • Factors that create the male-supremacist ideological superstructure
  • The hypocrisy of Bourgeois morality
  • The otherization of women
  • How masculinity defines objectivity
  • Motherhood as ideological subjugation of women in Class Society
  • Children's oppression in bourgeois society
  • There is no readymade revolutionary sexuality
  • Pornography as an expression of male supremacy
  • Objectification, sexualization and self-surveillance of women
  • Gender as a laborious process that demands unending conformism
  • Gender transition as a tool of re-socialization and knowledge production
  • Masculinity and femininity as ideological illusions of patriarchy

    • NOTABLE AUTHORS:
    Claudie Broyelle, Nathaniel Dickson, John Berger, Jim Griffin, Muskaan (from the Marxist Reading Circle)

    Section 6 - The Struggle for Bread and Roses

    In our largest (and ongoing) section, we have been studying about the political struggle for women and trans-queer people’s emancipation. This section is much more than just studying about the state stripping off women and trans-queer people of their legal rights and learning about how to organize for immediate tactics as well as long-term strategies for the women and trans-queer emancipation movements.

    Here we also read about the shortcomings of early Marxist theory on the question of patriarchy and the misogynistic, male-chauvinistic practices in the communist movement that hinder us from becoming responsible communists.

    Moreover, since we are more than halfway through the reading series at this point, in this section we aim to read all our texts in a sincere and critical manner.

    THEMES:
  • Denial-of-education as a tool of oppression
  • Denial of political rights in both private and public sphere—every single right is hard won(and recent)!
  • The history of women's emancipation movement during the French Revolution and the Paris Commune
  • The woman’s question as a part of Class Struggle in the socialist movement: how to organize?
  • Understanding the socialist origins of the International Working Women's Day (March 8)
  • The New Democratic Women’s Movement in India: strategy and praxis
  • A revolution is just the beginning
  • Socialist Queer Liberation Movement
  • Condemning sexual metaphysics and queer-antagonistic revisionism
  • Anti-Patriarchy Rectification Campaigns for masses and revolutionaries
  • Rethinking the history of patriarchy in pre-capitalist modes of production and capitalism
  • A political-economic analysis of reproductive labour
  • Immediate and long-term tasks of Socialist Revolution and New Democratic Revolution for women, trans and queer liberation

    • NOTABLE AUTHORS:
    Silvia Federici, Leopoldina Fortunati, August Bebel, Eleanor Marx, Clara Zetkin, CPI(ML)[People's War]

    Section 7 - Life Under Socialism

    Our upcoming section discusses accounts of the anti-patriarchal struggle and the women’s, children’s and trans-queer emancipation in former and current actually-existing socialist countries.

    It is not a rosy picture, however. We will discuss the progress as well as the shortcomings of the anti-patriarchal struggle, compare approaches of different countries, and reflect upon what needs to be changed as per our material conditions today.

    THEMES:
  • Necessity of continuation of Class Struggle and struggle for women and trans-queer emancipation after New Democratic and Socialist Revolutions
  • Progress of women, trans and queer emancipation former and current Socialist experiments/countries
  • Case Study: Progress of the women’s liberation movement in the Cultural Revolution of China during the 1970s
  • How the family ceases to be a family in the true sense of the term under Socialism
  • Understanding the nuances of Socialist sexual morality

    • NOTABLE AUTHORS:
    Claudie Broyelle, Anna Louise Strong, Foreign Languages Press Peking

    Section 8 - Conclusion: Half the Struggle, Half the Sky

    Finally, we will conclude this series by understanding how a sincere application of the socialist struggle to the question of patriarchy will emancipate women, children and trans-queer people from the shackles of slavery.

    THEMES:
  • From slavery to the dictatorship of the proletariat
  • Conclusion: the struggle continues

    • NOTABLE AUTHORS:
    Xie Jin and Liang Shin, Foreign Languages Press Peking
