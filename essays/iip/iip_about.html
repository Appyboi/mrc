The Ideological Illusions of Patriarchy: Masculinity and Femininity

About

Muskaan (they/them) is a Marxist-Leninist-Maoist from Ahmedabad, India. After spending all their childhood and teenage years with a burning passion for equality and justice, they discovered the science of Marxism and the communist movement at the age of 19 years via exposure to some comrades on social media in 2021, and have never looked back since. After being involved with a couple of organizations for a brief period of time each, they now work with the Marxist Reading Circle. This is their second sincere attempt at writing a theoretical text, and the first that they feel confident enough to put out in public.

This short treatise aims to perform a historical materialist and Marxist epistemological analysis on patriarchy. It intends to understand masculinity and femininity as the ideological tenets of gender that maintain patriarchal oppression in class society by assigning a false consciousness of political power to men and subservience to women. Furthermore, it aims to look into the ramifications of the same in the lives of women and trans-queer people, as well as considerations for the communist movement.

This text was originally intended for being read in the Marxist Reading Circle, hosted by the author, in January 2026. It is written as an original piece of work from the Marxist Reading Circle’s “Marxism on the Question of Patriarchy: A Comprehensive Study” reading series. This softcopy is intended for public accessibility afterwards. Should you wish to circulate digital or printed copies of this work, please send an email to marxistreadingcircle@gmail.com.

CONCEPTUAL PREREQUISITES: The reader does not need to know Marxist epistemology in advance as I will explain concepts such as ideology and ideological illusions in the text. However, one must have an understanding of basic dialectical and historical materialism, base and superstructure and the relation between the two, and basic Marxist political economy concepts such as labour-power, wages, capital, etc. Knowledge of the stages of primitive communist society mentioned in Com. Frederick Engels’ Origin of the Family, Private Property and the State (explained in Chapter 1: “Stages of Prehistoric Culture”) and some prior knowledge of social reproduction will also be beneficial.

NOTE: This text contains multiple mentions of gendered violence, especially domestic violence, female genital mutilation and r*pe. Sections with disturbing information are preceded by a trigger warning.
